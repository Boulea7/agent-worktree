# Evidence Boundaries

## Purpose

This file defines what may enter public specifications and what must remain in research notes.

## Allowed In Public Specs

- explicit user-facing contracts
- stable terminology
- documented compatibility claims
- clearly marked intended behavior
- carefully scoped future directions

## Allowed In Public Research Summaries

- synthesized conclusions
- documented tradeoffs
- compatibility comparisons
- reasons for adopting or deferring an idea

## Must Stay Out Of Public Specs

- raw AI-generated research dumps
- unsupported performance numbers
- claims without durable sources
- exact internal algorithms before implementation exists
- private workflow details or workstation-specific instructions

## Handling AI-Generated Inputs

AI-generated concept documents may be used as:

- brainstorming input
- structuring input
- terminology inspiration

They must not be treated as authoritative evidence until their claims are validated.

## Evidence Labels

Public documents should prefer one of these postures:

- `Adopted`
- `Planned`
- `Experimental`
- `Research`
- `Deferred`

This keeps readers from confusing intention with proof.
